# Sales Performance Analysis (SQL Project)
A SQL-based analysis of sales, customer behavior, and product performance.

## Problem Statement
This project analyzes sales, customer behavior, and product performance to understand overall business trends.


## Dataset
This dataset contains order, product, customer, and sales details. It was used to explore patterns in sales performance, customer activity, and profitability.


## Tools Used
- SQL (SQLite)


## Key Insights
1. Sales peaked in April 2020, indicating a strong performance spike compared to other months.

2. The dataset shows a lack of strong repeat customers, with most customers placing only one or two orders, indicating low customer retention.

3. The Tables sub-category within Furniture has the highest quantity sold (1303), indicating strong demand compared to other products.

4. Although some categories generate higher sales, profit margins differ across categories, showing that high revenue does not always mean higher profitability.


## SQL Queries Used
The main SQL queries used in this project are stored in the `queries.sql` file.


## Conclusion
This analysis shows that business performance depends not only on sales volume, but also on customer retention, product demand, and profitability. Looking at both revenue and profit helps provide a more complete view of performance.
